Fun Facts

  • In 1987. for the first time our shelter adopted a snake - the Saharan Black Mamba, which was found lost on the streets of Belgrade.
  • The shelter has so far found a new home for more than 23000 animals.
  • In November of 2016 out shelter was visited by the well-known actress and animal rights advocate Kristen Bell.
  • In a collaboration with The Philharmonic Orchestra of Belgrade, during 2017 and 2018 six concerts were organised to help raise funds for the renovation of some parts of our shelter.

About Us

The animal shelter "Riska" was founded back in 1956 in Belgrade by renowned veterinerians and benefactors Petar Jovanović and Marina Savić. Our humble beginnings have been captured in photos you can see in the gallery below.

In 1976, once the importance and benefit of our shelter for the local community was recognized by the relevant institutions, the shelter was given a brand new workspace at Bulevar umetnosti 129, where it has stayed ever since. In the following years, the shelter significantly grew its capacity and became a leading establishment of its kind in Serbia.

Today, "Riska" shelter takes in more than 2000 animals yearly - dogs, cats, birds and sometimes even other animals. Our shelter is organised according to the latest global standards and our staff work night-and-day to provide the animals everything they need, while maintaining a healthy and safe environment for them to live in.

Our website has won dozens of awards, including the "International Digital Emmy Award", "Webby Award" and others. Thank to our website, hundreds of people find their lost pets every year.
